# Silent Escape

## Short Description

Silent Escape is a 3D stealth horror prototype developed in Unity that explores AI-driven gameplay through environmental awareness, player detection, and dynamic tension. Instead of relying on combat mechanics, the experience is built around observation, evasion, and reactive enemy behaviour.

---

## Overview

The project was designed to investigate how gameplay systems, enemy perception, and environmental feedback can work together to create a tense and immersive player experience.

The core gameplay revolves around navigating hostile environments while avoiding detection from an enemy capable of reacting to both visual and auditory stimuli. Every gameplay system was developed with modularity and iteration in mind, allowing mechanics to evolve throughout development without tightly coupling their implementation.

## Lessons

Silent Escape became an opportunity to move beyond isolated gameplay mechanics and focus on building interconnected systems that support player experience.

During its development I improved my understanding of:

* Designing modular gameplay systems.
* Building scalable AI using state-driven behaviours.
* Combining visual and audio perception into a unified detection system.
* Creating gameplay-driven audio interactions instead of purely decorative sound effects.
* Structuring Unity projects for maintainability and future expansion.
* Iterating on mechanics based on playtesting rather than initial assumptions.
* Balancing technical implementation with player experience and game feel.
* Applying clean object-oriented design principles within Unity.

The project reinforced the importance of treating gameplay as a collection of independent but connected systems, making future iterations significantly easier while improving code readability and maintainability.
